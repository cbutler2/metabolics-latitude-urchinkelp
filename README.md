# Comparative Thermal Performance of Kelp and Herbivores Across a Latitudinal Gradient in Ocean Temperature
Data and code in support of the manuscript.

Citation: Butler, C., Hurd, C. L., Vergés, A., Ling, S., & Bennett, S. (2026). Comparative Thermal Performance of Kelp and Herbivores Across a Latitudinal Gradient in Ocean Temperature. Ecology and Evolution, 16(7). https://doi.org/10.1002/ece3.73910.


Overview of files: centro_rates.rds = absolute and mass standardised respiration rates and wet weights of individuals of the sea urchin, _Centrostephanus rodgersii_ sampled across multiple temperatures between 5 and 35 degrees Celcius at six sites across the latitudinal distribution of this species on the southeast coast of Australia. These data were obtained over a 7 week period.

helio_rates.rds = absolute and mass standardised respiration rates and wet weights of individuals of the sea urhcin _Heliocidaris erythrogramma_, sampled across multiple temperatures between 5 and 35 degrees Celcius at six sites across the latitudinal distribution of this species on the southeast coast of Australia. These data were obtained over a 7 week period.

eck_rates.rds = absolute and mass standardised photosynthetic rates and wet weights of individual sporophytes of the kelp _Ecklonia radiata_, sampled across multiple temperatures between 5 and 35 degrees Celcius at six sites across the latitudinal distribution of this species on the southeast coast of Australia. These data were obtained over a 7 week period.

1_ecklonia_glmm_metaregression.R, 2_urchin_lmmComparison.R, 3_urchin_polynomial_massInd.R, 4_urchin_polynomial_massStd.R = statistical R code used in the analysis presented.
